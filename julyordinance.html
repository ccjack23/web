July Ordinances

On the 26th of July, 1830, Charles X signed the July Ordinances. Through this order, he suspended the freedom of the press, dissolved the Chamber of Deputies, and disenfranchised the middle class, at the time the overwhelming majority of voters. Although Charles X claimed that he followed the Charter of 1814, signed by Louis XVIII to guarantee people the same rights that enjoyed during the French Revolution, at the time Charles X was never gonna give up the primitive values that was only acceptable over 40 years ago.

The Ordinances brought the opposite effect as it intended to do. The suspension of freedom of the press led to gatherings of journalists from various publications. On the right side of the page, you can see Le National, the largest newspaper publication in France, being seized by soldiers while journalists were complaining about the king's new policies. The dissolution of the Chamber of Deputies ironically let the liberal majority of the chamber have a foothold on installing a new king. Charles X, as tone deaf as he was, intended the July Ordinances to suppress the people, but it only fueled the simmering fire of the liberal forces in France.
